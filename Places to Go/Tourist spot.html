1.The CN Tower


CN Tower, also called Canadian National Tower is a broadcast and telecommunication tower in Toronto. It is Toronto's most iconic landmark with the tallest freestanding structure in the world from 1976, when it was built, to 2007 for over 30 years.

Location: 290 Bremner Blvd, Toronto, ON M5V 3L9, Canada

3. Royal Ontario Musuem


The Royal Ontario Museum (ROM) is Canada's pre-eminent museum in Toronto. It is the largest museum in Canada to explore both the natural world and humankind's cultural past. It has a collection of 13 million artworks, cultural objects and natural history specimens.

Niagara Falls is a spectacular waterfall in the Niagara River. It is the world’s greatest waterfall by volume at 2,832 m3. The Falls are stunning during the day and nighttime. It is best to visit in the Summer especially at night when it is lighted by fireworks and lights.
