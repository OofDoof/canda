Top 5 Famous Drinks In Canada


1. Nanaimo Bars Recipe

The Nanaimo bar is a bar dessert that requires no baking and is named after the Canadian city of Nanaimo in British Columbia. It consists of
three layers: a wafer, nut, and coconut crumb base; custard icing in the middle;
and a layer of chocolate ganache on top.

Main ingredients: Crumb, icing, chocolate

Rating:


2. Jo Louis Cake

Jos Louis is a Canadian confection consisting of two chocolate cake rounds with a cream filling within a milk chocolate
shell, made by Vachon Inc. It resembles a chocolate version of the May West dessert.
It was created in 1932 and named after two of the Vachon sons, Joseph and Louis

Rating:


3. Pouding chômeur

This Canadian cake, pouding chômeur, actually translates to “unemployed man’s pudding” or “poor man’s pudding”. It was created during the
Great Depression in Quebec, Canada by female factory workers

Rating:


4. Newfoundland Snowballs

The east coast of Canada sure knows how to make a dessert! The Newfoundland snowball is a Christmas tradition from Newfoundland. It is a small, round,
coconut-covered chocolate ball that will make your mouth come alive with the perfect blend of flavors.

Rating:


5. Butter Crunch

Butter Crunch is a Canadian dessert classic. This rich butter toffee is infused with maple syrup, and chopped walnuts and smothered in creamy
milk chocolate. It is then sprinkled with more delicious walnuts to give it some extra crunch.
